Trigonometry Made Simple: Sine, Cosine, and Tangent Explained

📅 2025-05-04 ⏱️ 8 min read 📐 MathMaster Blog

Trigonometry is the mathematics of triangles, waves, and cycles — and it shows up everywhere from architecture and music to GPS systems and medical imaging. The three fundamental ratios — sine, cosine, and tangent — relate the angles of a right triangle to its side lengths. Once you master these, you can find unknown sides and angles in any right triangle, model periodic phenomena like sound waves, and solve problems in physics and engineering. The name comes from Greek: trigonon (triangle) + metron (measure). With the right mental model and practice, trigonometry becomes one of the most satisfying parts of mathematics.

SOHCAHTOA: The Foundation

In a right triangle with an acute angle θ: Sine (SOH): sin θ = Opposite / Hypotenuse. Cosine (CAH): cos θ = Adjacent / Hypotenuse. Tangent (TOA): tan θ = Opposite / Adjacent. The mnemonic SOHCAHTOA helps you remember all three. Example: in a right triangle with hypotenuse 10 and opposite side 6, sin θ = 6/10 = 0.6, so θ = arcsin(0.6) ≈ 36.87°. The adjacent side = √(10²−6²) = 8. So cos θ = 8/10 = 0.8 and tan θ = 6/8 = 0.75. Key special angles: sin 30° = 0.5, cos 60° = 0.5, sin 45° = cos 45° = √2/2 ≈ 0.707, tan 45° = 1.

Key Point: Memorize the unit circle values for 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, and 90° — they appear in every exam.

The Unit Circle and Key Identities

The unit circle (radius = 1) extends trig beyond right triangles to any angle. A point on the unit circle at angle θ has coordinates (cos θ, sin θ). This is why sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 for all θ — it is the Pythagorean theorem on the unit circle! Other fundamental identities: tan θ = sin θ / cos θ. Reciprocal functions: csc θ = 1/sin θ, sec θ = 1/cos θ, cot θ = 1/tan θ. Signs by quadrant (ASTC rule — All Students Take Calculus): Q1 all positive, Q2 sine positive, Q3 tangent positive, Q4 cosine positive. Knowing reference angles (always measured from the x-axis) lets you find any trig value from Q1 values.

Solving Triangles and Applications

Given any two pieces of information about a right triangle (one side + one angle, or two sides), you can find everything else. Steps: identify which trig ratio connects your known and unknown quantities, set up the equation, and solve. For non-right triangles, use the Law of Sines: a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C, and the Law of Cosines: c² = a² + b² − 2ab cos C. Real applications include finding building heights from shadows, calculating distances in navigation, and analyzing wave patterns in physics.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 1
A ladder 5m long leans against a wall making a 60° angle with the ground. How high up the wall does it reach?
sin 60° = height / 5 height = 5 × sin 60° = 5 × (√3/2) = 5 × 0.866 = 4.33 meters
Exercise 2
In a right triangle, the adjacent side is 7 and the hypotenuse is 25. Find all angles and the opposite side.
cos θ = 7/25 → θ = arccos(0.28) ≈ 73.74° Opposite = √(25²−7²) = √(625−49) = √576 = 24 Other angle = 90° − 73.74° = 16.26°

Conclusion

Trigonometry is a gateway to advanced mathematics, physics, and engineering. The three ratios — sine, cosine, and tangent — are simple fractions with extraordinary power. Build your foundation with SOHCAHTOA, then expand to the unit circle and the fundamental identities. With daily practice on varied triangle problems, you will develop intuition for which ratio to use and how to set up each equation quickly and correctly.
